]]> 3 Common Hard Disk Problems and How to Fix Them

 

Whether you’re on a mac or a PC, it doesn’t matter, the hard drive is one of the most important components of a computer. A corrupt hard disk can disrupt your work making it nearly impossible to use your PC.

 

Hard disk usually starts to fail when it starts accumulating bad sectors after being used for a long period of time. The complete hard disk failure is hard to detect since the symptoms match with that of virus and malware infection.

 

These symptoms usually slow down the computer or start corrupting your files, rendering them useless. But the worst part is that hard disk failure is almost sudden and there is very little possibility of recovering full data. If you have ever encountered a problem that you suspect is due to your old hard disk, don’t worry.

 

Geeks on Repair has established itself as a company that fixes these hard disk errors for our clients. But we also believe it is important to educate them so they can troubleshoot common disk errors at home.

 

In this post, we will list down the 3 most common hard disk problems we see at our store and how to fix them at home.

 

Error 1 - Hard Disk Not Found

 

  • There are times when you turn on your system and you see a message saying that the ‘hard drive was not found’ on your screen. This prevents your system from starting and sometimes you can’t even use the typical commands to get out of the screen.

 

This error usually occurs when your hard disk is not connected properly to the motherboard through cables or the connector of the HDD is loose. Water or physical damage to the HDD can also cause this error to appear.

 

  • Solution - The simplest way to get past this error is to shut down your system and open your PC case to see if the cables are working properly. You may also want to check for physical or water damage. If the connection is loose, just tighten it up and boot the system again.

 

Error 2 - Volume is Dirty

 

  • Sometimes you may get an error while copying a file that says your disk or volume has been corrupted. The problem can occur on both external and internal hard drives. This is due to a bad sector on your HDD, or if you are using an external drive, the file system may not be supported.

 

Solution - If you are seeing this error more times than usual, it is a good idea to see if your hard drive has bad sectors. To find out, simply run the Windows disk error checking tool by right-clicking on the drive icon in your system.

 

  • You may also want to try and reconnect your external hard drive to see if the disk starts working again.

 

Error 3 - Unable to Boot Operating System

 

  • The hard drive stores the firmware of your system which means if it gets into trouble, you might have problems booting your system up. There are all kinds of on-screen errors that tell you of this when you power on your system.

 

If the operating system does not start, there could a problem with your BIOS settings, or it could mean essential system files present on the hard drive have been lost or corrupted.

 

  • Solution - The best way to deal with the Hard disk problems is to enter the BIOS of your motherboard and restore the factory settings. After that, restart your system and if anything was wrong with the BIOS settings, they will be fixed.

 

Or if you are an advanced user, you can boot your system in command prompt and try to recover OS files or completely reinstall the OS. You’ll need a bootable USB drive containing important system files to do this.

 

Get Professional Help for Hard Disk Problems

 

  • If these did not solve your issue, there could be a bigger problem with your hard drive. In times like this, it is always a good idea to have your hard disk checked by professional computer repair services.

 

If you continue to use a hard disk with bad sectors, it may completely fail at some point making it impossible for you to recover your data. Before this happens, bring your system to a computer repair store and get it fixed.

]]> At Geeks on Repair, we see all sorts of viruses come through our shop. While we gladly help our customers with virus removal services, we also always try to educate them on how to prevent viruses from infecting their PCs in the first place.

 

Below, we have provided 5 very simple and smart ways to prevent viruses from taking refuge in your system. As a starting note, we also want you to note the difference between malware and a virus.

 

What is a Computer Virus?

  • A virus can best be described as malicious computer software that copies itself into your computer and infects it. Viruses can easily be transferred to your system through local private/public networks, the internet, or through a USB/external hard drive.

Here at Geeks on Repair, we have dealt with so many virus removal jobs where the virus was actually hidden in the customer’s flash drive the whole time. As soon as we cleaned their system and they went home, the virus would come back when they connected their flash drive.

 

What is Malware?

  • Malware is a program that is made to deny operation on a system. It can also gather information from a system, or gain access to crucial system resources.

Malware is a very tricky program that takes extra effort to remove. Typical anti-virus programs do not have the right tools to detect and remove malware, so it is very important that you do everything in your power to prevent it from infecting your system.

 

5 Smart Ways to Prevent Viruses From Infecting Your Computer

Here are some ways you can prevent viruses and malware from infecting your system.

 

Keep Windows Up to Date

  • Since Microsoft Windows is susceptible to viruses and malware more than most other operating systems, it is important to keep it updated. I know how annoying it is to see an ‘update’ window popup every time you turn your system on, but it is important to let it do its thing.

Many of the updates released by Microsoft for Windows 7,8 and 10 include crucial security patches that fill security loopholes in the system. And not to mention, viruses keep evolving, so the team at Microsoft has to keep updating the system to fight back new and complex viruses.

 

Use Only One Anti-Virus Program

 

  • We’ve met a lot of customers in our shop that has multiple anti-virus programs running on their computer. While it may seem that having multiple anti-virus software is a good defense against viruses, unfortunately, it’s not.

Anti-virus programs have special instances running in the background that check every new thing being added to the system. From the pictures to the music and video files, whenever something is added to the system, the anti-virus program checks it before it is added.

  • When multiple such programs are running in the background, they can start to interfere with each other which can be taxing for your system resources. So in other words, stick to a single program and keep it updated at all times.

 

Scan Email Attachments

 

  • A lot of people get their systems infected via scam emails. Hackers send scam emails with clever email addresses that convince the user to download attachments from the email or click on malicious links.

With a good anti-virus program, you can easily scan email attachments and protect your computer from being infected.

 

Get Malware Scanner

 

  • Malware scanners are different from anti-virus programs and you should at least have one of them running in the background. Since anti-virus software can’t detect and remove malware, your only hope of getting rid of one is by using the anti-malware program.

The good thing is that most anti-malware software is free to use, so don’t hesitate to have one on your system.

 

Use a Trusted Browser

 

There are many internet browsers out there and you should choose the one that provides the best security features. We recommend using Google Chrome or Firefox for most of your browsing needs.

]]> The worst of the worst has happened, and you’re left with a broken computer.

Situations like these can be extremely stressful, and you’re already frantically typing “computer repair near me” into your phone.

Luckily, we’ve compiled some of the best tips to assess potential businesses for proper computer repair. It’s important that you think carefully about your service provider ahead of time to ensure that your device is in the best hands possible.

1. What Services Do You Need?

Before seeking out proper computer repair, you’ll want to determine precisely what services you need. For example, if you’ve done proper troubleshooting beforehand, you’re not going to have to pay for basic data backup or file transfers.

If you have the time, try to use the internet to determine exactly what the issue is. A quick Google search of your device’s symptoms can bring you to a bevy of helpful online forums that may or may not lead you closer to a solution.

Plus, a good search can help you eliminate any causes that do not seem plausible. Therefore, you’ll have more information to give your prospective technician, saving you time and money.

 

2. Does this Business Receive Good Reviews?

While reviews aren’t everything, they can certainly give you a better idea of a prospective repair company’s quality of service. It also doesn’t take a whole lot of extra time to look up reviews online, but it could save you a lot of hassle in the long run.

For the best results, look for crowd-sourced review sites. Most of these sites do not allow the companies themselves to leave reviews, making them a great unbiased source. Try to cross-reference a couple of sites just to be safe.

In addition, pay special attention to any mentioning of good customer service. This is a hugely important factor for reasons we’ll discuss later on, but regardless, keep an eye out for it.

 

3. Any Onsite Options?

Many repair places nowadays offer onsite repairs which can be super convenient and time-saving. However, oftentimes, this service comes at an extra cost. If you need someone to come to you, budget accordingly.

If you’re unsure of whether or not your potential service provider offers onsite service, go ahead and give them a call.

 

4. What Can You Do On Your Own?

Before calling any repair place, research on how to do some basic troubleshooting from home. This can involve backing up data appropriately or simply using the task manager to help diagnose your computer’s issue.

While troubleshooting may not be your strong suit, it’s certainly an important skill to develop. Plus, backing up data alone preemptively can save you money.

 

5. How Is Their Customer Service?

Possibly the most important factor to pay attention to is how your prospective repair company prioritizes customer service. Any respectable service provider knows that customer care should be at the top of the list, so don’t settle for mediocre technicians.

Technicians who genuinely care about their customers are much more likely to provide quality service since they have more skin in the game. They’re also more likely to go above and beyond to make sure that you’re 100% satisfied with your service.

If you’re not sure exactly how a service provider will perform, give them a preemptive call and see how they respond to your questions. Should they be anything less than friendly and informative, seek help elsewhere.

 

6. What Are Their Pricing Options?

Before calling any prospective repair companies, decide upon a reasonable budget. Remember, just because you’re inquiring about a service, doesn’t mean you don’t have any bargaining power. If you need a reference point while creating your budget, look on a couple of company websites. A lot of times, there will be set price point menus to give you a better idea of where to start.

Once you have your budget, call your prospective repair technician and relay your budget. See if their willing to work with you on any price cuts. In addition, sometimes you can get a price break from a referral bonus.

If you can, ask your friends and family for any local recommendations. Should they have any specific companies or technicians they work with, make sure to mention their names when you have your initial consultation. If you don’t get a price break, at least you’ll get an extra layer of accountability ensuring top-notch service.

 

7. Who Can You Ask For Help?

If you haven’t already, ask friends, family, or anyone you can think of for service recommendations. Talking to someone during a stressful time can be cathartic, regardless of the subject, plus, they can certainly point you in the right direction.

Try to ask for specific names of technicians in order to boost your chances of getting a referral bonus.

8. What Questions Have You Prepared?

Before making your initial call to a potential computer service company, you’re going to want to have a list of questions prepared for the best possible service results.

In addition to getting you your needed information, asking questions will help you discern whether or not a service provider is up to a high standard. Pay attention to how the technician approaches answering your questions.

Do they answer thoroughly and politely? Or do they struggle to provide proper answers? Testing a company’s upfront knowledge is a great way to gauge their future quality of service.

 

Only Settle For the Best

Finding a proper computer repair place isn’t easy by any means, but it is certainly achievable. Just remember the next time you’re looking up “computer repair near me”, you’re following these sound tips to find the best service advice possible.

Remember, you never have to settle for mediocre service. Great computer repair technicians are out there, you just have to know how and where to look.

]]> Picking out a new TV can be exciting.

If you’re lucky, you’ve scored a black Friday deal and gotten your TV set at a fraction of the regular price. However, now that you have your TV, how do you find a professional TV installation company? And more importantly, how do you find quality service that will protect your initial investment for years to come?

Luckily for you, we’ve done our homework and come up with a couple of tell-tale signs of an excellent installation company. Consider this your checklist for evaluating potential service providers.

With a little extra patience and research, you’ll be sure to find the perfect installation company for your needs.

Qualified Certifications

Any proper installation company will have a number of certifications authorizing them to work on a variety of products. If your propective company isn’t able to prove their credentials, look elsewhere.

You can also look on the manufacturer’s website of your TV to see authorized service dealers. By doing this, you’re putting in the extra work to ensure you find an excellent installation company.

 

Customer Service

If you aren’t receiving great customer service, even during the initial consultation, look for another installation company. You should never have to settle for poor service, and any respectable place of business knows the importance of putting their customers first.

Plus, great customer service is also an indicator of excellent service. The more a company cares about the individual, the more likely they are to put in the effort to properly secure your device. Make excellent customer service a priority of your professional installation company search.

 

Organization

Is your prospective installation company neat, clean, and organized? Or do you find them flipping through paper manuals, drowning in unnecessary paper?

Any great company is organized as this speaks to their level of professionalism. Moreover, an organized company is more likely to be on top of their payment structure and will provide reliable service.

You deserve the absolute best so any installation company that is giving you any less in terms of their organization skills is not worth your time.

 

Included Services

Do you have a smart TV? What devices are you looking to connect to your TV? If you need help connecting other devices in your home, look for installation companies that are well-rounded in service.

Some companies will include connecting devices or organizing cables for example, as part of their service. Make sure you specify desired additional services during your initial consultations with potential installation providers.

 

Mounting

When installing your TV, perhaps one of the most important factors to consider is whether or not you’re going to want your TV to the wall. In this case, you’ll need to purchase a TV mount ahead of time that can support the weight and length of your device.

Some companies specialize in wall mounting, so be sure to look out for this handy feature. If you don’t have a mount yet, look for installation companies that provide custom mounts as a part of their service.

These services are likely to be more expensive but can be a great help if you don’t want to take any chances with your wall mount for your new TV.

 

A Willingness To Inform

Come up with a list of questions beforehand to ask your installation company. During your initial consultation, ask your prospective installation vendor your questions.

If they do not answer thoroughly or pass off the questions entirely, they are not worth your time. Any great installation service has a willingness to inform their clientele, not just offer a one-off service.
You should feel super comfortable asking your technician as many questions as you need. If you feel any negative pressure, look somewhere else.

 

Local Service

Local installation service companies are not only more convenient, but they are likely to offer up better service packages than larger tech conglomerates who have a large base of clients they have to appeal to.

Individualized service is almost always superior, and this is something that you’ll only likely find in a smaller service vendor. Don’t be deterred by their potential lack of overall reach. Rather, use the other qualifying factors on this list to determine whether your potential TV technician is well equipped for the task at hand.

Not to mention, local service providers are likely to get you your service faster. As they say, time is money, and this is an important factor to consider while comparing multiple service providers.

 

Budget Negotiations

]]> Your worst nightmare has come true:

Your computer has stopped working for one reason or another, and you need a repair fast. However, just because your computer isn’t working doesn’t mean that the world stops turning. You still have plenty on your to-do list and responsibilities to attend to. It seems your only option is to settle for a repair shop nearby.

Luckily for you, we’ve compiled a list of helpful ways to pick out the best local computer repair company possible. With these 10 tips, you’ll get your computer repaired in no time at all without adding extra stress to your life.

 

1. Take a Deep Breath

There’s no denying that a broken computer can be stressful, to say the least, but try your best to take a deep breath. Stress can cloud your focus and make it more difficult to find a suitable repair facility. Know that eventually your computer will be repaired or replaced, and you’ll be able to resume with regular life very soon.

 

2. Phone a Friend

One of the best ways to relieve stress and hear about nearby computer repair shops is to call beloved friends and family members. Tell your friend about your computer, and see if they have any helpful recommendations. Should they have a computer repair place in mind, ask to see if they worked with a particular technician.

Sometimes, computer repair shops will offer referral bonuses to their customers allowing the new and old client to both get a discount. You could end up saving money for both you and your friend. Make sure you ask your friend about the customer service of the prospective repair company. It’s likely that well-treated customers also receive well-treated computers, so make this factor a priority.

 

3. Read Online Reviews

If you don’t have the time to call up a friend or if your search is fruitless, check out online reviews for a couple of prospective companies. Make sure you use crowd-sourced review sites like Trustpilot or others to ensure that you’re receiving unbiased accounts.

Just as you would with a personal referral, look for depictions of the company’s customer service.

 

4. Research Your Computer

One of the best ways to prepare yourself for a successful computer repair is to know your device inside and out. Read up all that you can on your computer including your specs, serial number, and model type.

If you’re unsure where to start, begin with a quick Google search. You can find most information on the manufacturer’s website, and there’s usually a hotline number available if you’d rather speak to someone over the phone.

Ultimately, the more you can learn about your device beforehand, the better. Not only will this help familiarize you with your laptop or PC, but you’ll also be able to help guide your future technician’s service.

 

5. Look For Certified Repair Shops

While on the manufacturer’s website, you should also be able to see certified repair service dealers. Though these services are sometimes more expensive, you’re likely to receive higher quality services.

Moreover, try your best not to go with a large tech repair conglomerate. Though these services may be pretty easy to track down, you’re not likely to receive the same level of individualized care that you would with a local computer repair company. In addition, these companies may charge high prices since they have a decent level of exposure. For your wallet and quality of service, seek out a smaller repair service.

 

6. Prioritize Remote Repair

Some repair technicians will come straight to your door to service your computer. This will no doubt save you lots of time and money, as you won’t have to travel at all. However, sometimes this convenience can come at a cost, so prepare your budget accordingly.

Look on potential repair candidates’ websites to get a better idea of their service options.

 

7. Budget Appropriately

Before your initial consultation, ask yourself how much you can afford. Remember, just because computer repair companies have suggested price points, doesn’t mean you aren’t able to negotiate.

Compare prices across company websites and use that information to your advantage. During your first phone call, ask for direct prices so you’re not blindsided with a large bill.

 

8. Backup Data

If at all possible, back up as much data as you can before seeking out computer repair services. Some companies will charge you extra to save your files when you can easily do this on your own with a little patience and research.

Should you not know where to start, make a quick Google search. There are tons of blog posts and informational videos out on the web about proper data backup. In the future, try to keep your important files on an external hard drive just in case something goes haywire. Not only will it give you peace of mind, but you’ll always have your files easily accessible in case you need to use a different device.

 

9. Troubleshoot Preemptively

After backing up your data, troubleshoot your computer with these basic tips. Sometimes, even just a little proactive troubleshooting can save you loads of time and money. Plus, you might learn more about your computer’s current state. Be sure to record all information gathered from your troubleshooting stage to relay to your repair technician. With this information, they are more likely to provide you with efficient service.
